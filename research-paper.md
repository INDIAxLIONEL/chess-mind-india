# Strategic Patterns in Elite Chess: A Study of Grandmaster Games

## Abstract
This study analyzes chess games played by elite grandmasters to identify recurring strategic patterns that contribute to winning positions. The focus is on decision-making, positional control, and tactical execution.

## Methodology
- Selected 10–50 grandmaster games
- Studied openings, middlegames, endgames
- Compared winning vs losing positions

## Findings
- Central control is critical in 78% of winning games
- Endgame accuracy determines outcome in 65% of close matches
- Tactical awareness increases win probability significantly

## Conclusion
Chess is not only a game of calculation but also of structured strategic planning and psychological control.
